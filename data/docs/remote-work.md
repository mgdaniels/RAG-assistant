# Remote and Hybrid Working Policy

*Owner: People Team — last updated 15 January 2026*

How we balance flexibility with time together in the office.

## Hybrid by default

Most roles are **hybrid**. You are expected to be in the office a **minimum of 2 days per week**. Our two **anchor days are Tuesday and Thursday**, when teams aim to overlap in person for collaboration and meetings.

To keep remote and in-office colleagues in sync, we observe **core collaboration hours of 10:00–16:00 UK time**, during which you should be reachable on Slack and available for meetings. Outside those hours you can flex your start and finish times.

## Fully remote working

Fully remote arrangements are possible **by exception** and require **director approval**. They are reviewed annually. Because of tax and right-to-work requirements, employees must be **based in the UK** unless a specific arrangement has been agreed in writing.

## Home office setup

New joiners receive a **one-off £200 home-office setup allowance**, claimed through ExpenseDesk. If you need ergonomic equipment (chair, monitor, keyboard), request it through IT rather than buying it yourself, so it can be tracked as company equipment.

## Working well remotely

- Keep your calendar up to date so colleagues can see your availability.
- Default to written updates in Slack and Confluence so remote colleagues aren't left out.
- If your home internet fails, the nearest office or a co-working day is a reasonable fallback — claim co-working costs through ExpenseDesk with manager approval.

Questions about hybrid arrangements go to **people@northwind.example**.
